What is Quantum Computing?

Classical computers use **bits** (0 or 1). Quantum computers use **qubits**, which can exist in a 0, 1, or both simultaneously.

Think of a bit like a coin lying on a table—it’s either Heads or Tails. A qubit is like a coin **spinning on its edge**. While it's spinning, it’s in a "superposition" of both states until you touch it and make it stop.

Why it Matters in 2026

  • Scale: Solves problems in minutes that supercomputers would take millennia to process.
  • Efficiency: Uses interference to "cancel out" wrong answers and amplify the correct ones.

Core Quantum Pillars

The engineering fundamentals of the subatomic world.

Superposition

The ability of a system to be in multiple states at once, allowing a computer to explore every possible solution path simultaneously.

Entanglement

Particles so deeply connected that the state of one instantly affects the other, regardless of distance. This is the "secret sauce" for secure comms.

Interference

Using wave-like properties to bias the quantum state toward the correct solution, effectively "muting" the noise of incorrect data.
